However, it is important to recognise the important role the faction of Free France played in the allied victory, particularly in the liberation of Axis-controlled African nations.

    Allies

    Winston Churchill (Great Britain)


    Sir Winston Leonard Spencer Churchill was a British statesman who served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1940 to 1945 (during the second world war). Churchill was born on 30 November 1874 at his family's ancestral home, Blenheim Palace in Oxfordshire, England. His father, Lord Randolph Churchill, served as an MP for Woodstock; his mother, Jennie Spencer Churchill, was a daughter of Leonard Jerome, a wealthy American businessman.


    Churchill was a member of the Liberal Party from 1904 to 1924, before joining the Conservatives the following year. He is widely considered to be one of the most prominent figures of the 20th century, and one of the most important leaders in the history of Great Britain - as he led the country to victory in the Second World War.






    Franklin D. Roosevelt (United States)


    Franklin Delano Roosevelt (FDR) was an American politician and attorney who served as the 32nd president of the United States from 1933 until his death in 1945. Roosevelt was born on January 30, 1882, in Hyde Park, New York, to businessman James Roosevelt and his second wife, Sara Ann Delano. They were sixth cousins and both came from wealthy New York families.


    FDR famously led the US into Second World War in 1941, following the Japanese Empire's attack on the Hawaii naval base, Pearl Harbor. Prior to this, the US did not want to get involved in the conflict in Europe as public support was low. However, they did provide the UK with supplies.





    Joseph Stalin (USSR)


    Joseph Vissarionovich Stalin was a Georgian revolutionary and the second leader of the Soviet Union (from 1922 until his death in 1953). During his premiership, he transformed the Soviet Union into a major world power (usually by implementing "5 Year Plans"). The industrialisation of the USSR and collectivisation of agriculture were his two main strategies - and, of course, police terror to help secure his position from any potential rivals or uprisings. Following Adolf Hitler's 'betrayal' in 1941 when Operation Barbarossa was launched (Germany's invasion into the Soviet Union), Stalin and the USSR joined the Allies and heavily assisted in the defeat of Nazi Germany.






    Axis

    Adolf Hitler (Nazi Germany)


    Adolf Hitler was born in 1889, in Braunau am Inn, Austria. His father, Alois, died in 1903 - however he was never fond of him. Instead, Adolf was devoted to his mother, Klara. Throughout his early life and time in education, Adolf aspired to become a renowned artist. He was however, rejected on two occasions to study this field. The beginning of The Great War saw Hitler defend the Central Powers. He was awarded with the Iron Cross, First Class in 1918 - which was a rare achievement for a corporal.


    Following The Great War, Hitler despised the Treaty of Versailles, which heavily limited the Weimar Republic in most ways. He joined the ational-sozialistische Deutsche Arbeiterpartei (Nazi Party) in 1919 (although it was not named this until the following year). He rose through the ranks until he eventually was made Chancellor of the Weimar Republic by Paul von Hindenburg (the state's then leader). He later assumed total control of the nation, turning it into Nazi Germany.


    As the leader, or Der Führer, Hitler released terror and hatred into Europe - commiting numerable atrocities in his wake to secure domination.




    Emporer Hirohito (Empire of Japan)


    Michinomiya Hirohito was born in 1901 in Tokyo, Japan at the Aoyama Palace. He was the son of the Taishō emporer and the grandson of the Meiji emporer. He became Emporer of Japan in 1926 and served as Japan's longest-reigning monarch until his death in 1989.


    Japan at the time was entering a stage of expansionism, invading neighbouring countries in order to secure resources that the Japanese mainland lacked in. Many believe Japan's actions in the late 1930s act as the beginning of the Second World War, and not Nazi Germany's invasion of Poland.





    Benito Mussolini (Kingdom of Italy)


    Benito Amilcare Andrea Mussolini was born in 1883 in Predappio, Italy. From an early age, Mussolini was disobedient, unruly, and aggressive. He was a bully at school, and as his teachers were unable to control him, he was sent to board with the strict Salesian order at Faenza. He proved to be more troublesome than ever during his time here.


    After initially falling in love with Marxism, Mussolini eventually became an avid 'antisocialist' and sought to create a new ideology in global politics - fascism. He was the first fascist leader (Il Duce) in Europe.
